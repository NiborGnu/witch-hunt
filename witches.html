Stor-Märit

The intense period of witch trials known as "the big uproar" started in Dalarna, where Gertrud Svensdotter, who was accused of walking on water in 1667, singled out Märit Jonsdotter from Lillhärdal as her teacher. Since the minutes from the trial in Lillhärdal are very long as the legal process took several years, we have selected parts of the text.
Read through the protocol and see what Märit, who was called Stor[Big]-Märit to distinguish her from her sister of the same name, Lill[Little]-Märit, was accused of and what she tells herself.

On September 28, Gertrud Svensdotter and a stranger, 17-year-old Anna Oloffsdotter, came from Loka in Älvdalen, to Lillhärdal parish. Both have previously confessed. Anna has never been in Lillhärdal previously. The court asks them if they recognized this Stor-Märit Jonsdotter. Gertrude answered that Stor-Märit had been her teacher and that she had ridden with her many times Blue hill. The other, Anna Olofsdotter, said that she cannot know for sure if she knows Stor-Märit or not because she has never here on earth seen her ever before. Therefore convened six of the neighbor maids here in the village, namely Margreta Persdotter, Kirstin and Märit Mattsdotter, Märit Olofsdotter, Märit Larsdotter and Elin Persdotter, who were the same age as the infamous Stor-Märit Jonsdotter. All were bareheaded and gathered together in a group. After this were allowed to go one by one to a small square window, 2½ blocks in size [about 37 cm], where Anna Olofsdotter stood alone. When the maids arrived one by one, Anna said that "I don't know that one", "No the” and so on. But when Stor-Märit came, Anna replied that "I have seen her many times in Blåkulla, sitting on the bench among the foremost at the high seat". Gertrud told the same thing, that she saw that Stor-Märit went with Satan to the chamber and into the bed where she committed fornication with him. After this, Satan had come out and put on Stor-Märit two large horns that reached all the way up to the roof and danced with her.

Lill-Märit Jonsdotter and her brothers, Olof and Jon Jonsson, was called to Länsmansgården. The were interrogated one by one in a chamber in Gertrud and Anna's presence. Although these three hard-hearted persons were tried to be induced to confess, even with God the almighty gracious help, they refused. In the end, the obstinacy was softened and they prayed together to God that the evil one the spirit would leave them. After the prayer, they burst into tears and confessed that they had been there quite often Blue hill. Little Mary had been misled by Big Mary. Olof and Jon had been misled by the father, Jon Skomakare who lived here in Lillhärdal parish, but who had died two years ago in Särna parish in Dalarna where he then lived and worked.

These siblings, Lill-Märit, Olof and Jon, fully admit that Satan had strong power over them and with threats prevented them from telling about their own or others' association with him. The siblings tells us that Stor-Märit is even more employed by Satan and has a stronger covenant with him. She has been in his service for a long time and the siblings fear that she will confess sooner or later. The who are still young or those who are old, who have served Satan for a long time, find it easier to confess. Those who are in their prime have a harder time getting rid of Satan. After Little-Märit, Olof and Jon's confessions, Great-Märit Jonsdotter was again brought in and told her what her carnal siblings had confessed, both about themselves and about her, how how many times she had been in Blåkulla, how often Lill-Märit had seen her fornicate with Satan and also how she saw Stor-Märit dancing with the big horns on her. Stor-Märit recoiled from this and did not allow himself to be influenced in the slightest but continues to deny and claims that the siblings are lying and do not know what they have confessed. Stor-Märit believes that the siblings have now abandoned God and been led into evil the road. The siblings fell into tears over Stor-Märit's stubbornness, took Stor-Märit in their arms and asked her to The name of Jesus drive Satan away and become a child of God.

Elisabetta

The largest witch trial in Sweden took place in 1674 in Torsåker parish, Ångermanland. When the trial was finished, 71 people, 65 of whom were women, had been sentenced to death. Elisabetta, aged 24, was one of these. The one who led the trial was the priest Hans Wattrangius.

As compensation he had received 1 Riksdaler, 1 silver spoon and 1 gold spoon from her. He had also received food from her.
