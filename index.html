The Witch Hunt in Sweden

The Great Noise

The Great Noise claimed hundreds of victims

During a few years between 1668-1676, hundreds of people were executed in Sweden for witchcraft. Most were well-established women who were accused of Blåkullafärder and consorting with the devil. The evidence was often based on the testimony of children.

Hundreds of years after the last witches bonfires have burnt out, there is still speculation as to why they were actually lit.

Was it a male medical profession that wanted to get rid of competing wise old ladies? Or were the witches drug addicts and members of secret cults that the government wanted to suppress? After all, if the historians are to be believed, the biggest culprits in the drama seem to have been a frightened public who were willingly supported by zealous priests.
